Project Invent and Team DREW mentor


Project Invent is a non-profit organization that partners with high schools around the world to empower students to invent technologies for social good.

Since February 2019, I've had the pleasure of being the design thinking + tech mentor to an all-female team of high school sophomores.

The first month of the mentorship was focused on teaching them the basics of design thinking and helping them craft their How Might We statement. In mid-March, they decided to help children with autism: "How can we help children with autism cope with stress and identify their emotions?"

Since then, they've been hard at work, following design thinking methods, researching, and self-studying Arduino (my C is poor, so we both struggled here) to create DREW, a tech-enabled teddy bear that aims help soothe children with autism during stressful situations.

You can find out more about DREW and the team on their website (The website also doubles as a way for me to teach them UI/UX and how to use HTML/CSS to create interfaces.)

In May 2018, during Project Invent's Demo Day, Team DREW was awarded the $1000 innovation startup fund to continue their efforts. (Photo by Chris Cassell, Pro Bono Photo)
